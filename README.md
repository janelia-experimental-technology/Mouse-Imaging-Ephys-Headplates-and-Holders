### Mouse Headplates and Holders for Imaging and Electrophysiology

Researchers at Janelia have designed several head plates for live mouse imaging and electrophysiology experiments in collaboration with jET. 



The head plates are attached to the skull of the mouse typically with dental cement, and are used to locate the brain to the microscope. 

The Barrel Ctx Plate (Sloped Left) Headpost was developed by and used extensively in the Svoboda lab for in vivo imaging of the barrel cortex and other cortical areas (e.g. Huber et al 2012). The Headpost with three epoxy holes was used by the Svoboda lab for electrophysiology experiments.
